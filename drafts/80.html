Mindfullness

Published: Fri 23 May 2014
By tushartyagi

In Uncategorized.

If I take a seat while waiting for someone, the first reaction is to pull the phone out of my pocket and start doing something on it; starting usually with checking the time. I look around and see other people doing that too; almost everyone who is alone is either listening to the music, looking into their phone screens; even if there is a group of people, chances are that they are doing something on their phone. It won't be wrong to say that a lot of people are just zoning themselves out by looking at the life of fantasy, past, future or someone else (Facebook, twitter) and then thinking about how good or bad it is?

The human brain has been designed to think continuously, we had been thinking and working our way out of obstacles, difficult and life-threatening situations ever since we were animals living in the forests. Then came the time when we had to think in order to hunt, kill, eat, make fire, discover new ideas, invent new things, and as a result we are living in a world full of comforts where almost everything can now be done at a click of a button, this is magical going by the standards of people living only 100 years ago.

Evolutionarily, all this thinking provided our brain with an auto-pilot mode for a lot of situations; this is good and required so I don't have to think too much when filling a glass of water and drinking from it, or taking a car out for a ride. But the problem with zoning out is that in a lot of situations I just do things without thinking about them at all, as if I was high.

For instance, when I'm playing an interesting video game, or reading an interesting book, the answer to every question that I'm asked becomes "Yes", and this causes a few surprises for me sometimes. The answer to both "Do you want to eat, I'm preparing something?" and "I'm going out, I won't cook today." becomes "OK", which is problematic in situations when the food is ready and I am already full, or when there's no food prepared and I'm famished. Another instance is when I just sit on a chair and keep thinking about the future or the past.

Mindfulness, by its virtue, is about living life in the present, wherein one's mind is full with whatever is going around him. It is about being more physically present in the moment, than being mentally present. So when you walk you can feel the ground, hear the tap of your shoes, feel the gusts of wind on your face, feel the cloth on your body. The time becomes a lot more slow, since you have so much to feel or observe in every instance. It becomes a walking meditation, bringing inner peace and calm, where the worries about the future and mistakes of the past find no place. The person becomes an observer of whatever is going around him; he passes no judgements, or thinks how he would do it differently, because doing that takes him away from the present moment.

But living in the present is pretty difficult, since we are made to think, to think about the past in order to learn from our mistakes, or to think about the future in order to make plans and do things.

In the past, that made a lot of sense. When fire was discovered, controlling it would have killed a lot of our ancestors. One of our ancestors was killed because he was curious the way bushes were moving that way and then was promptly killed by sabre toothed tiger. So learning from the past served us to survive over the millenia.

Thinking about the future made us more open to new ideas, I imagine the early humans saw that a few of their mates were killed after falling over large and sharp stones, and they imagined what it would be like to throw them at the enemies. Newton was possibly thinking about the future when apple fell over his head (or in front of him, whatever the case).

But that much thinking isn't really needed for every hour of every day, the life expectancy of humans have increased from a 30 to around 65, we are no longer surrounded by so many threats and while the technology is moving at an exponential rate, it's not going to help us to think about the future too much at an individual level.

I've found that I have a habit of zoning out every now and then, daydreaming about situations and while studies prove that it is good for the brain and sparks creativity, and if I take a bus I know that I'm not the only one doing that (one quick check is to see if the other person is looking through you, into the infinite), and some of the ideas that I have come from daydreaming and letting my mind go off the way, but that isn't something that I want to do for a majority of time, after all dreaming always feels better than doing.

Thinking too much about the past might depress us, and thinking too much about the future might make us anxious, and that's why mindfulness becomes important because it forces one to live in the present. The word forces is important since this mode of living is difficult to be in because of evolutionary wiring and because the whole idea of present is so difficult to grasp because of it's transitive nature. It only comes a moment at a time, unlike the past and the future which have a more "block" like structure and are more easier to think about or imagine. Unlike the past and the future, the present quickly becomes long, boring or difficult.

The advantages of mindfulness are though, multiple:
Improved concentration
Reduced anxiety
Reduced depression
Too many chances of getting into the flow
Good ideas might come up more frequently
The way I try to be more mindful is:
Starting small, trying to be mindful in the tasks which only takes a few seconds at a time, but occur very frequently or everyday like eating, drinking, walking to the kitchen, toilet, in my room etc.
Meditation
Exercise
Mindful walks few times a week
Be more mindful, whenever the thought of being mindful comes up. It's a cycle, be more mindful and the mind comes up with the idea of being mindful.

The brain can be taught to do so many things, and I've noticed that once I start being mindful more often throughout the day, the idea of being more mindful keeps on coming up again and again
