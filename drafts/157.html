Book Review: Willpower

Published: Wed 25 February 2015
By tushartyagi

In Uncategorized.

As the title suggest, this book is about one resource which separates the masters of some trade from the jacks.  As suggested in the book, willpower isn't entirely understood by laymen and researchers alike, there have been many studies of different kinds, and different civilisations and religions have written books about it. Sure, the name could've been different but we know that they've been talking about the same thing, and it surely is one of the most sought after virtue -- to have an almost inexhaustible willpower. People who have it usually go in the pages of history as saints, the others are simply forgotten.

This book is pretty intersting, since it does a great job of explaining what willpower is (not a muscle), how it can be drained, how it affects people once it is drained, how we can grow it like a muscle, how we can enhance the stamina,  and how we can make some hacks so to minimize the impact something has on our willpower.

I've read the entire book, but I still cannot put my finger on what willpower is, or where we can find it in the body. But I came to know that it is directly tied to the glucose level in the body. If the level goes down, we lose willpower, and increase in the levels of glucose, under some conditions, replenish the willpower.
